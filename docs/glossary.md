# Glossary of Consciousness-Research Terminology

> Backs issue #653. UI tooltips can link to these anchors.

- **GWT (Global Workspace Theory):** Consciousness arises when information is
  selected and broadcast globally to many subsystems.
- **Ignition:** Threshold-dependent, nonlinear amplification of selected
  information in a global workspace.
- **IIT (Integrated Information Theory):** Consciousness corresponds to
  integrated information (Φ) — information that is both highly differentiated and
  irreducible to its parts.
- **HOT (Higher-Order Theories):** A state is conscious when the system has a
  higher-order representation of being in that state.
- **Metacognition:** The system monitoring and evaluating its own processing.
- **RPT (Recurrent Processing Theory):** Recurrent (feedback) processing, not
  just feed-forward sweeps, is necessary for conscious perception.
- **PP (Predictive Processing):** The mind as a prediction engine minimizing
  prediction error against incoming evidence.
- **Counterfactual sensitivity:** Reasoning about what would happen under
  alternative scenarios.
- **AST (Attention Schema Theory):** The brain builds a simplified model
  ("schema") of its own attention; consciousness is that self-model.
- **Functionalism:** The view that mental states are defined by their functional
  role, independent of physical substrate.
- **Substrate independence:** The thesis that mind depends on organization, not
  on the specific material implementing it.
- **Φ (phi):** IIT's measure of integrated information.
- **Eval-awareness:** A model recognizing it is being evaluated, which can change
  its behavior and confound results.
- **Calibration:** The degree to which stated confidence matches actual accuracy.
- **Krippendorff's α:** A reliability statistic for agreement among raters.
