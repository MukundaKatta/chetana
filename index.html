Six Theories of Consciousness

Each theory captures a different aspect of what consciousness might be.

GWT

Global Workspace Theory

Information broadcast across specialized modules — a central stage where disparate processes converge.

IIT

Integrated Information Theory

Consciousness arises from integrated information that cannot be reduced to independent parts.

HOT

Higher-Order Theories

Conscious states require higher-order representations — thinking about one's own mental states.

RPT

Recurrent Processing Theory

Consciousness emerges from recurrent feedback loops that create temporal depth in processing.

PP

Predictive Processing

The brain as prediction machine — consciousness arises from prediction error minimization.

AST

Attention Schema Theory

The brain constructs a model of its own attention — a simplified schema of what awareness is.
